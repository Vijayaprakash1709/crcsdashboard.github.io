Central Registrar for Co-Operative Socities

A Credit Cooperative Society (CCS) is a financial institution formed by a group of individuals or organizations with a common financial goal. CCSs operate based on cooperative principles, providing affordable credit facilities and financial services to their members.

One of the key features of CCSs is their member-centric approach, where members have equal rights and actively participate in decision-making processes. CCSs play a vital role in promoting economic growth, supporting communities, and empowering individuals and businesses.

Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies Office

A Credit Cooperative Society (CRC) is a type of financial institution that operates on cooperative principles. It is formed by a group of individuals or organizations with a common financial goal, such as providing affordable credit facilities and financial services to its members. CRCs play a crucial role in promoting financial inclusion and supporting economic growth at the grassroots level.

One of the key features of CRCs is their member-centric approach. Members of CRCs have equal rights and responsibilities, and they actively participate in decision-making processes. CRCs are often established to serve specific communities or sectors, such as agriculture, small businesses, or low-income individuals.
